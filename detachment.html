Core Systems
Eight tools.
One goal.
01
🔥
Streak System
The behavioral core of the app. Every day without initiating contact adds to the streak — a visible number that makes relapse feel costly. Milestones at 7, 14, 30, 60, and 90 days. Urges and profile-checking don't break it. Only reaching out does. If a relapse happens, the user resets, reflects on what triggered it, and starts again. The reset is not a punishment — it's a data point.
Core mechanic
02
🔒
Reality Vault
After a breakup, the brain edits memory — softening the painful parts, amplifying the warmth. The Reality Vault counters this. At the start, users record red flags, incompatibilities, painful moments, and lessons while the truth is still raw. When nostalgia hits, the app surfaces these entries in the user's own words — before the brain had time to rewrite history.
Anti-idealization
03
🛡️
Memory Shield
Many setbacks are triggered by environment — a photo in camera roll, an old chat, a memory notification. Memory Shield moves photos to a hidden vault without deleting them. Accessing them requires passing a mindfulness gate first: "Are you in the right state of mind?" The goal is not to block, but to interrupt the impulse long enough for rational thinking to take over.
Trigger reduction
04
📓
Journal Log
Recovery without reflection is just waiting. The Journal Log creates a daily habit — users record their mood, urges, triggers, and progress. Over time, patterns emerge: certain times, situations, and emotions that consistently precede urges. This data feeds the Guidance Engine. Looking back at day three and comparing it to day forty-five is one of the most powerful motivators to keep going.
Daily reflection
05
⚡
Guidance Engine
Not a chatbot — an observer. The Guidance Engine reads streak length, journal entries, urge frequency, and time of day, then delivers one targeted message at the moment it's most needed. A reflection at day eighteen when urges peak. A reminder at 11pm when late-night impulses are highest. Short, calm, and specific. It doesn't lecture. It simply reminds you of what you already know.
Context-aware AI
06
🤝
Community
Isolation makes heartbreak worse. Knowing that thousands of others are going through the same process — and coming out the other side — makes it bearable. Users anonymously post milestone moments and recovery reflections. No usernames, no likes, no performance. Just real entries from real people. Bootstrapped with curated content at launch so it never feels empty from day one.
Peer recovery
07
🎙️
Vent
Sometimes you're too overwhelmed to write. Vent lets you speak or type freely — raw, unfiltered, no structure required. Everything goes directly into your Journal Log, transcribed and timestamped. It captures the emotion in the moment before it fades or gets rationalised away. The journal doesn't need to be polished. It just needs to be honest.
Express freely
🎤
Voice Vent Hit the mic and talk. Your words are transcribed instantly and saved directly to your journal — no typing required.
✏️
Text Vent Open a blank page and just write. Stream of consciousness, no prompts, no judgment. It lands straight in your log.
🔒
Fully Private Vents are never shared, never analysed publicly. They exist only for you — a pressure valve with no audience.
08
🎓
Friends Who Moved On
Once you've genuinely healed, the app offers a choice. You can delete your account and close the chapter. You can archive your journey as a private record. Or you can become a Friend Who Moved On — a recovered user who reaches back for someone still in it. Not a formal mentor. Just one person who made it through, offering the kind of support only lived experience can give.
Post-recovery
🤲

🗓️
Your Terms Async messages or live sessions. Mentor on your own schedule, in your own way.
